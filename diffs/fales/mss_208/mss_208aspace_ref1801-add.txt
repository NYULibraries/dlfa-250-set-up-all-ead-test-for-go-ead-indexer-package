     <field name="bioghist_teim">American chemist Eben Norton Horsford (July 27, 1818 - January 1, 1893), was the only son of missionary and politician Jerediah Horsford and his wife Maria Charity Horsford of Moscow, NY. In addition to Eben, Jerediah and Maria Charity produced four daughters: Maria Horsford Byam, Julia Horsford Norton, Eliza Horsford Tryon, and Laura Horsford. </field>
     <field name="bioghist_teim">Eben earned a civil engineering degree from RPI (Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute) in 1838 and contributed to the Geographical Survey of New York under the supervision of geologist and RPI paleontologist James Hall. Between 1840 - 1844, Eben lectured at Delaware's Newark College, in addition to teaching mathematics and natural sciences at the Albany Female Academy, where he would meet sisters Mary L'Hommedieu Gardiner and Phoebe Dayton Gardiner as students. Between 1844 - 1847, Eben studied with chemist Justus Freiherr von Liebig in Giessen, Germany and returned to the U.S. to marry Mary L'Hommedieu Gardiner and serve as the Rumford Professor and Lecturer on the Application of Science to the Useful Arts at Harvard University, where he would steadily publish and teach until 1863. </field>
     <field name="bioghist_teim">Mary gave birth to four daughters: Mary Leila (Lilian) in 1848, Mary Katherine (Kate) in 1850, Gertrude Hubbard in 1852, and Mary Gardiner (Mamie) in 1855. Mary died a few months after the birth of Mamie. Eben wed Mary's sister Phoebe in 1857, and the couple produced one daughter, Cornelia Conway Felton, in 1860. </field>
